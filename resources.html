Resources

EMDR: The Breakthrough Therapy for Overcoming Anxiety, Stress, and Trauma. Book by Francine Shapiro.

This book is a great explanation for EMDR and the ways in which it can be applied written by its creator Francine Shapiro. The 3rd edition of this book was published 20 years after the 1st edition and includes many research studies that has been conducted about the effectiveness of EMDR.

EMDR International Association

A comprehensive resource to find explanations and guidelines about EMDR therapy, as well as EMDR therapists in your area.

No Bad Parts: Healing Trauma and Restoring Wholeness with the Internal Family Systems Model. Book by Richard C. Schwartz.

This is the latest book by the creator of IFS, Richard Schwartz. I find this book to be very powerful and very easy to read.

The Larger Self. Article by Richard C. Schwartz.

This is an article written by the creator of IFS, Richard Schwartz, and it is a shorter read than his book but very touching as it tells the story of the author’s first encounter with parts in a client.

Building a Life Worth Living. Book by Marsha M. Linehan.

This book is the autobiography of Marsha Linehan, the creator of DBT. I find this book remarkable not because of its prose, but because it allows you to understand how Marsha Linehan created one of the most influential approaches to therapy, the obstacles and invalidation she suffered, and the coping skills she used to overcome them.

Full Catastrophe Living. Book by Jon Kabat-Zinn.

A powerful guide to mindfulness, full of drops of wisdom. I believe Jon Kabat-Zinn to be the missing link between Eastern Zen philosophies and Western medicine.
